This article is a prerequisite to the [Projects](/pages/collection-projects) and [Routines](/pages/collection-routines) collections.

Both projects and routines:
- Consists of a set of tasks.
- Both can fall under the realm of NEED and WANT to do.

Projects and routines are two distinct units of tasks that have different purposes, structures, and lead to different outcomes.

## An accouting system for your resources.

Routines and projects are the line items in your resource budget.

To manage your projects and routines well is to manage your resources well. → To manage your resources well is to manage your self well.

## Order of operations.

1. Routines: the FIXED expenses in your resource budget.
2. Projects. the DISCRETIONARY expenses in your resource budget.

## Why you will not find a 'task' collection in the Extended Mind System.

There is no to do list, task manager, or dedicated collection to tasks because every single task rolls up to a project or routine.

So we manage things at the project and routine level not the task level.

This is not an idea I came up with on my own. It was heavily influenced by David Allen's views on projects in Getting Things Done.

## Why you will not find a 'goals' collection in the Extended Mind System.

We don't do goals around here. We do systems (routines) and projects (you define the end state and meet it).

Goals are aspirational. Where as routines and projects are a commitment to consistent inputs and a desired outcome / end state.

## On leisure hours.

You'll notice moving through the the following pages there is no accounting for liesure hours.

People tend to think of routines and projects and default to work, chores, and things we have to do not thing we WANT to do. 

I want you to update your mental model. Routines and projects are not synonymous with work. They represent units of time and a collection of tasks that have different purposes, structures, and lead to different outcomes. They are tools for budgeting your resources – predominately, time. And just as funds get allocated to fun purchases time can get allocated to fun activities.

This is also a good time to remind you that the extended mind system is not a PRODUCTIVITY system in the sense of we're trying to maxmize your work hours and make you a busy bee. It's about deciding who you are, what is important to you, and ensuring you are dedicating your valuable resources to what you have determined to be your priorities.

So liesure can actually fall into the realm of routines or projects: reading a book, having brunch with friends, planning and taking a trip, learning a new hobby or skill, building something with your kids just for fun, etc.

But my general philosophy on leisure: it should serve to make yourself better (which can include resting), nourish and improve relationships, or leave the world better than you found it. At a minimum, don't use your time to make things worse – remain neutral if you aren't going to focus on nourishment and improvement.
